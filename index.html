Endeavor

My professional focus is privacy infrastructure for artificial intelligence: the engineering layer that turns privacy requirements into systems developers can use, operate, and verify.

My long-term endeavor is to advance privacy infrastructure for artificial intelligence: systems, tools, and engineering patterns that make it possible to understand, constrain, and verify how sensitive data moves through AI products.

As GenAI systems become more capable and more deeply integrated into consumer and enterprise products, privacy work needs to move beyond one-off reviews into reusable infrastructure. That includes data lineage, policy enforcement, asset classification, developer workflows, and runtime controls that scale with product velocity.

Why It Matters

AI expands the data surface area

GenAI systems can depend on large, complex, and fast-changing data flows. Privacy work has to account for training data, retrieval, evaluation, logging, feedback loops, and downstream product use.

Governance needs infrastructure

Policies become durable only when they are embedded into developer workflows, data systems, enforcement services, observability, and runtime controls.

Scale changes the problem

At large product scale, manual review alone cannot keep pace. Teams need reusable primitives for lineage, classification, purpose limitation, access boundaries, and auditability.

Technical Map

I think about AI privacy infrastructure as a system of connected layers. Each layer has to be understandable to developers, enforceable by platforms, and measurable by privacy and governance teams.

01

Lineage, asset discovery, data classification, sensitivity signals, and data-flow visibility.

02

Policy translation

Turning privacy requirements into machine-readable constraints, product rules, and platform contracts.

03

Enforcement and guardrails

Purpose limitation, access controls, runtime checks, workflow gates, and safe defaults for AI builders.

04

Observability and assurance

Auditing, monitoring, explainability for data movement, compliance evidence, and operational reliability.
